Eiffel Tower, Paris, France

Overview

  • Location: Champ de Mars, Paris, France
  • Height: Approximately 330 meters (1,083 feet)
  • Construction Period: 1887-1889
  • Architect: Gustave Eiffel

History

Purpose: Built as the entrance arch for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World's Fair), held to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution.


Controversy: Initially criticized by some of France's leading artists and intellectuals for its design, it has since become a global icon of France and a symbol of romance and adventure.

Louvre Pyramid, Paris, France

Overview

  • Location: Cour Napoléon, Louvre Museum, Paris, France
  • Height: Approximately 21.6 meters (71 feet)
  • Base Width: 35.4 meters (116 feet) on each side
  • Construction Period: 1984-1989
  • Architect: I. M. Pei

History

Purpose: Commissioned by French President François Mitterrand, the Louvre Pyramid was built to serve as the main entrance to the Louvre Museum and to facilitate better visitor flow.

Notre-Dame Cathedral, France

Overview

  • Location: Île de la Cité, Paris, France
  • Height: Approximately 69 meters (226 feet) at the top of the towers
  • Construction Period: 1163-1345
  • Architect: Multiple architects including Jean de Chelles and Pierre de Montreuil

History

Purpose: Notre-Dame Cathedral was built to serve as the main cathedral of the Archdiocese of Paris and a place of worship for the Catholic faithful.

Imperial Palace, japan

Overview

  • Location: Chiyoda, Tokyo, Japan
  • Area: Approximately 1.15 square kilometers (0.44 square miles)
  • Construction Period: Edo Castle built in 1457, Imperial Palace established in 1868
  • Architect: Multiple architects over the centuries, with various restorations and expansions

History

Purpose: The Imperial Palace serves as the residence of the Emperor of Japan and a central location for important ceremonies and state functions.

Ueno Park, Tokyo, Japan

Overview

  • Location: Taito, Tokyo, Japan
  • Area: Approximately 0.53 square kilometers (0.2 square miles)
  • Established: 1873
  • Management: Tokyo Metropolitan Government

History

Purpose: Ueno Park was established as a public park to provide recreational space and to promote cultural and educational activities. It was one of Japan's first public parks.

Tokyo tower, tokyo, japan

Overview

  • Location: Minato, Tokyo, Japan
  • Height: 333 meters (1,092 feet)
  • Construction Period: 1957-1958
  • Architect: Tachū Naitō

History

Purpose: Tokyo Tower was built as a communications and observation tower to support television broadcasting and offer panoramic views of Tokyo.

Statu of Liberty, New York, USA

Overview

  • Location: Liberty Island, New York Harbor, New York, USA
  • Height: 93 meters (305 feet) including pedestal
  • Construction Period: 1875-1886
  • Architect: Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi (sculptor) and Gustave Eiffel (engineer)

History

Purpose: The Statue of Liberty was a gift from France to the United States, symbolizing freedom and democracy. It was dedicated on October 28, 1886.

Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York, USA

Overview

  • Location: 1000 5th Ave, New York, NY 10028, USA
  • Area: Over 2 million square feet of gallery space
  • Established: 1870
  • Management: Metropolitan Museum of Art Board of Trustees

History

Purpose: The Metropolitan Museum of Art, also known as "The Met," was established to encourage and develop the study of fine arts and to collect and exhibit artworks.

central park, New York, USA

Overview

  • Location: Manhattan, New York, NY, USA
  • Area: 3.41 square kilometers (843 acres)
  • Established: 1858-1876
  • Designers: Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux

History

Purpose: Central Park was created to provide a green oasis in the heart of Manhattan for recreational activities and to improve the quality of life for New York City's residents.
